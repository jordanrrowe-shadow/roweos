# Brilliance — Investor Framework (Skeleton)

**Status:** Deferred. Skeleton only. **Do not pursue investors in v33.0.**
**Purpose:** When the moment is right, this is the structure. Filling it in is a separate session, separate week.

---

## Why this is a skeleton

Brilliance has 10 paying clients, ~$500/month MRR, a single founder, and a Google for Startups acceptance. v33.0 is the moment the product *becomes a brand*. The right moment to talk to investors is **after** v33.0 ships and the platform has a coherent face — when the deck doesn't have to qualify itself with "this is RoweOS, soon to be Brilliance."

Premature investor outreach with the rebrand mid-flight signals chaos. Patient outreach with a launched, coherent Brilliance signals discipline.

This document captures the structure so that when the moment arrives, the work is not greenfield.

## When to revisit this doc

Trigger conditions (any of):
- v33.0 has been live for 30+ days with no major regression
- MRR > $5K with >25 active paying customers
- Inbound interest from a credible investor or strategic partner
- Personal financial trigger (Jordan needs the runway)

Anti-triggers (don't pursue if any apply):
- v33.0 has unresolved bugs affecting paying clients
- Brilliance positioning is still drifting between "consumer AI" and "enterprise platform"
- Less than 12 months of personal runway evaporated (desperate fundraising loses leverage)
- No interest in giving up board seats / control

## The pitch in one sentence

> Brilliance is the intelligence layer for operators who run multiple brands and refuse to lose their voice in someone else's chatbot.

## The pitch in three sentences

> Generic AI gives generic answers. Brilliance learns who you are — your brand voice, your standards, your obligations — and injects that identity into every response before the model sees it. The result: AI output that compounds in your style, across brand work and personal life, with you owning the keys.

## Positioning

**Category:** Sovereign AI for operators (NOT "AI assistant", NOT "no-code agent platform", NOT "ChatGPT for X")

**Vs. competitors:**
- vs ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini consumer apps → identity-first, multi-brand, sovereign-by-design
- vs Lindy/Cluely/Glean → consumer-grade, not enterprise-only, single-operator focus
- vs Notion AI / Monica / superhuman → not bolted onto another tool; an OS, not a feature
- vs Replit Agents / Cursor → for non-technical operators, not engineers
- vs no-code workflow tools (Zapier, Make) → AI-native, not glue
- vs DIY (LLM API + scripts) → consumer-grade UX, sovereign architecture

**Why now:**
- Operator economy has 50M+ creators / SMB owners managing multiple brands
- AI tools assume a single brand or are stuck in "team chat" enterprise pricing
- Identity is the moat AI can't sell: it's user-supplied, user-owned
- Sovereign AI (BYOK, browser-direct routing) is a credible response to "AI vendors will train on my data"

## The 11-slide deck (when ready to build)

1. **Cover** — Brilliance / Intelligence OS / by RoweOS / firefly Brilli mark / contact
2. **The problem** — Operators run multiple brands. Generic AI flattens their voice. Existing AI platforms force a choice between consumer simplicity and enterprise pricing.
3. **The insight** — Identity is the part of AI that doesn't generalize. The model is commodity; the context around it is the moat.
4. **The product** — Brilliance: brand and life intelligence layer. Two modes (BrandAI, LifeAI), one operator. Sovereign by design. Cross-mode intelligence.
5. **Demo** — Brilli in action. Studio writing in your brand voice. Pulse showing your day. Tax Copilot referencing your brand revenue automatically. (live demo or 60s video)
6. **The traction** — current MRR, active clients, retention, NPS. Google for Startups credential.
7. **The market** — operator economy size, AI spend trajectory, sovereign-AI category emergence. TAM / SAM / SOM.
8. **The model** — pricing tiers, gross margin per tier, current per-user costs, path to $X ARR.
9. **The moat** — identity infrastructure (user data we hold by permission, not extraction), cross-mode intelligence (single platform spans brand + life), sovereign architecture (positioned for the privacy backlash), zero-framework codebase (rapid iteration without team scale).
10. **The team** — Jordan (CEO/founder/builder), advisors, hiring plan.
11. **The ask** — round size, use of funds (people, not infra), milestones, terms.

## Use of funds (template)

Typical seed structure ($1-2M):
- 40% engineering hires (1 senior, 1 mid)
- 25% design / product (1 senior designer + contract motion designer)
- 20% growth (content, paid acquisition experiments, partnerships)
- 10% infrastructure (compute headroom, observability, security audits)
- 5% legal/admin

## Risks investors will ask about

| Risk | Honest answer |
|---|---|
| Single founder | Solo for now. Hire plan is engineering first. Co-founder TBD if right person. |
| BYOK is a friction point | Yes. Mitigated by Brilliance-supplied key option for non-technical users. |
| Model providers could vertical-integrate (OpenAI launches identity layer) | Possible. Counter: identity owned by user, portable across providers; consumer trust accrues to neutral platform. |
| Multi-brand niche is too small | Disagree. Solo operators with portfolios are 10M+ globally. Brand-of-one creators are 50M+. |
| AI commoditizes the wrapper | Wrapper is the wrong frame. Identity infrastructure is the substrate. |

| 10 clients is small | True. Plan is to use seed funding to grow methodically (not aggressively) — this is a craft business that scales by quality not virality. |

## Strategic alternatives to outside funding

**Bootstrap to $50K MRR** before considering investors. Possible if:
- Pricing structure can support it (current Founder tier may need to grow)
- Acquisition is repeatable (need a working channel; word-of-mouth alone caps growth)
- Founder runway lasts (12+ months minimum)

**Strategic partnership / acquisition by Anthropic, Google, etc.** — only if at much larger scale, otherwise undervalues the identity moat.

**Service revenue from The Rowe Collection brands** — Rowe Solo Training, Retreats, Reserve, &Co are real businesses that can fund Brilliance development. Defensible diversification.

## What NOT to do in the deck

- Don't lead with the tech stack (vanilla HTML, no framework). Investors don't reward unusual choices; they reward outcomes.
- Don't quote Cluely-like ARR claims. Honest numbers.
- Don't position as "AI for everyone." Position as "AI for operators who refuse to compromise."
- Don't promise features in the next 12 months that haven't been built. Show what's shipped.
- Don't compare to competitors directly. Define a category Brilliance owns.
